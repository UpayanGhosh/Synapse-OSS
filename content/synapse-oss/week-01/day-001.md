# Day 1 — What I'm building and why I built it on WhatsApp

## Script (90 seconds)

I wanted an AI that knows me. So I built one.

Not a chatbot. Not a thin wrapper around ChatGPT. An actual assistant living in my WhatsApp, speaking Banglish with me, remembering what I told it three months ago.

The idea came from frustration. I was switching between AI tools constantly — Gemini for this, Claude for that, local models when I didn't want cloud APIs anywhere near my personal stuff. None of them knew who I was by the next conversation. Every session was a reset.

So I started building Synapse. I'm in Kolkata, working on this after hours, one piece at a time.

What it is now: a multi-channel AI assistant running on WhatsApp, Telegram, Discord, and Slack simultaneously. It routes every message through 8 processing stages before the LLM ever sees it. It learns from conversations and rewrites its model of me every 50 messages automatically. It runs on my laptop — 8GB RAM, mostly local models, cloud fallback for tasks that need more power.

15,000 lines of Python. 302 tests. 11 subsystems. 4 channels. 6-plus LLM providers.

This took 12 months. Some of it worked immediately. A lot of it didn't. Over the next year I'm going to break down every decision, every bug, every thing I rebuilt from scratch.

Follow if you want to see how this evolves.

### LinkedIn

I got tired of AI tools that forget who you are between sessions.

Every conversation starting from zero — re-explaining your context, your communication style, your preferences — before you could get anything useful done. I speak Banglish (Bengali written in English, mixed with actual English). Most tools treat that as a fringe case. For me it's just how I communicate.

So over the past 12 months, working evenings after my day job in Kolkata, I built Synapse. It's a personal AI assistant that runs locally on 8GB RAM, lives across WhatsApp, Telegram, Discord, and Slack simultaneously, and learns from every conversation through a pipeline I'm calling Soul-Brain Sync — which rewrites its model of me automatically every 50 messages.

15,000 lines of Python. 302 tests. 11 subsystems. Open source.

Over the next year I'll be breaking down every piece of the architecture — the decisions that worked, the ones that didn't, and the things I had to rebuild completely.

Every message passes through 8 stages before the LLM sees it: flood gate (3s batching) → dedup (5-min TTL) → task queue (max 100) → workers → context assembly → LLM → send.

Each stage exists because something broke without it.

**[3/4]**
It learns from conversations via Soul-Brain Sync.

Every 50 messages, a batch process distills the history into 8 profile layers — core identity, linguistic patterns, emotional state, domain knowledge, interaction style, vocabulary, exemplars, meta context.

That profile compiles into a 1500-token system prompt segment. Automatically.
