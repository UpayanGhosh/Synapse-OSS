# Day 6 — How I plan big projects alone: phase gates with PLAN.md

## Script (90 seconds)

I shipped a 15,000-line project alone. Here's how I stayed sane.

Not Jira. Not Notion. Not a spreadsheet. A text file called PLAN.md and a rule I set for myself: nothing gets merged until VERIFICATION.md says it's done.

Every chunk of work starts with a PLAN.md — what I'm building, what it connects to, what "done" actually means. Not vague. Specific. "The FloodGate buffers messages from the same user for 3 seconds and releases them as a batch to the task queue." That level of specific.

Then I build it.

Then I write VERIFICATION.md — a record of what I checked, what passed, and what came up that I hadn't planned for. If something fails verification, it doesn't move forward. I write a gap-closure plan and address it.

This process evolved out of necessity. Early in the project, I'd ship things that worked in isolation but silently broke something else. Without a verification step, I wouldn't catch it until later — and later is more expensive to fix.

The downside is it's slower. Some weeks I shipped nothing because I was writing plans and verification documents.

But 12 months in, I can add a new feature without breaking three other things. The architecture is still coherent. I can explain any part of it without looking at the code.

That's the trade. I think it was worth it.

- **Visual suggestion:** Show the file structure — PLAN.md + VERIFICATION.md in the repo, or a split showing the plan on one side and the verification on the other
